About ASTERON

ASTERON is a U.S.-based optical interconnect brand focused on serving the North American market with application-driven, supply-chain-enabled connectivity solutions.

Company Overview

Operated by Gnem Inc. in Los Angeles, California, ASTERON is built to connect qualified manufacturing capability with the needs of North American customers. Our business is centered on optical modules and interconnect solutions for AI infrastructure, cloud data centers, telecom operators, system integrators, and networking vendors.

Rather than positioning ourselves as a vertically integrated manufacturer, we operate as a focused market-facing brand and commercial platform—combining product selection, supply-chain coordination, branding flexibility, and customer engagement under one U.S.-based interface.

What We Do

ASTERON delivers optical connectivity solutions through a qualified ecosystem of manufacturing and technology partners. Our role is to align product capability with real market demand—helping customers source, brand, evaluate, and deploy optical products that fit their application, timeline, and commercial model.

Who We Serve

  • AI Data Centers
  • Cloud Infrastructure Operators
  • Telecom and Carrier Networks
  • System Integrators
  • Networking Vendors
  • Channel and Distribution Partners

Working Model

ASTERON supports multiple cooperation models depending on the target market and customer requirement. These may include ASTERON-branded supply, selected distribution programs, private label opportunities, and project-based sourcing support.

This flexible structure enables us to respond to a wide range of deployment scenarios while maintaining a consistent customer-facing experience for North American buyers and partners.

Brand Vision

ASTERON is built around a simple idea: optical connectivity should be commercially accessible, technically credible, and ready for the next generation of network demand. Our mission is to help customers move faster—from evaluation to deployment—through a stronger connection between global optical capability and local market execution.
